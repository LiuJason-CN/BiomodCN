Fabrication of DNA catenanes

All DNA strands were synthesized and purified by Life technologies. Heat the mixture of three single-stranded DNA to be catenaned (0.5μM) and their corresponding splints (1μM) in 1X T4 DNA ligase reaction buffer (19.5μL) to 90℃ and incubate for 3 min. Cool down to 25℃ (0.1℃ /s) and kept for 20 min. Add 0.5μL of T4 DNA ligase (2.5U) and incubate at 25℃ for 2 hours. Inactivate enzyme by heating the mixture in water bath at 90℃ for 3 minutes. Load reaction solution of 2 μL onto a denaturing polyacrylamide gel.

Gel electrophoresis

Three-ring catenanes were analyzed on an 11% denaturing polyacrylamide gel under 350V and five-ring catenane were analyzed on a 6% denaturing polyacrylamide gel under 300V. The gels were run in 1X TBE buffer at ambient temperature. Samples were prepared as below:

AFM

(AFM) measurements were performed at room temperature using a multimode scanning probe microscope with a Nanoscope 3A controller (Digital Instruments/Veeco Probes/USA). AFM images were recorded on freshly cleaved mica surfaces, prepared by the deposition of a 10 μL sample of the respective DNA nanostructures. The simples were dried in the air. Images were taken with MPP-11100-70 tips (Veeco) using the tapping mode at their resonant frequency.
