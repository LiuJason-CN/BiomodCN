DNA Triad

The mechanism of formation of DNA Triad is more intriguing than fabrication. In the original design, the existence of sister band of two-ring catenane (Lk 2) is because two strands prefer to braid with each other rather than stay separately after forming a full helix. ssDNA can form double helix not only in the base pairing sites but also in the mismatched region, once a basic helix was formed by 6 or more matched oligonucleotides. The unexpected mismatch (Lk 2) blocks the binding of the third LDNA with other two pre-rings, and further affected the assembly of Triad. Whole process can be shown in Figure 5



Figure 5 Mechanism of low yield of ABC

The insertion of TT spacer perfectly solves the problem. Two-ring catenanes of A1BC can barely be observed in Figure 1, lane 4-6. This is because the TT spacer impedes DNA to form double helix when complementary region is less than 10 bp. Since one turn of B-DNA is 10.5bp, 8bp is not enough to catenate two strands together. Therefore, the yield of two-ring catenanes is very low and no binding regions of the third strand are taken up so that no difficulty stands on the way of forming Y-shape DNA, as shown in Figure 6



Figure 6 Mechanism of TT spacer effect

Catenation is hard to happen when there are only two strands in the reaction system. But once we add the third strand to the system; the catenation process becomes efficient and productive. This is because the third strand plays as ‘splint’ role- it recruits two strands together in a close distance and make them catenate. What’s more, the third strand prevents two strands from forming catenane with two topological crosses and thus, less byproduct was observed in the reaction systems.

The experiment results prove the mechanism, shown in Figure 7. We took one of connectors out from the solution when we fabricate DNA Triad. Thus only two-ring catenanes could be produced (lane 2, 5, 8). For catenane ABC, additional strand do not affect the yield of two-ring catenane. However, catenanes with two topological crosses were eliminated under the presence of the third strand. For catenane A1BC and A2BC, the influence was even more striking. Compared with lane 5, 8, almost no two-ring catenanes were observed in lane 4, 7. The results suggest that formation of DNA Triad could only be performed in presence of three strands and explain why yield of DNA Triad improved after adding TT spacer into the strand.



Figure 7 Fabrication of two-ring catenane with or without presence of the third strand. (a) catenane A(A1, A2)B (b) catenane A(A1, A2)C (c) catenane BC. Lane 1, 4, 7 contain two-ring catenanes made without the third strand. Lane 2, 5, 8 contain two-ring catenanes made with the third strand. Lane 4, 6, 9 contain three-ring catenane ABC, A1BC, A2BC respectively.
