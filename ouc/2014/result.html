Fabrication of DNA Triad


Figure 1．Denaturing PAGE patterns of DNA Triad (a) ABC, (b) A1BC and (c) A2BC. Lane 1-3 contain rings made from single strand, A (A1 or A2), B and C, respectively. Land 4-6 contain catenane made from strand A (A1 or A2) +B, strand A+C, strand B+C, respectively. Lane 7 contains catenane made from strand A (A1 or A2) +B+C.



Figure 1 a-c is the denaturing PAGE pattern of DNA Triad. The samples were loaded in the same volume on the 11% denaturing gels for characterization of DNA catenane. After optimizing the catenation methods, the highest yield of Triad was obtained when three pre-ring LDNA were added simultaneously (shown in Materials and Methods) and all samples were prepared in that way.

We originally assumed that no other byproduct could be seen if catenation efficiency was high enough. However, as shown in Figure 1a, the yield (40.4%) of three-ring catenane leaves much to be desired. Furthermore, individual two-ring catenane exhibit varying catenating patterns (lane 3-6, Figure 1a).Sister bands which have lower mobility than main product were observed on the pattern. Both bands disappeared upon linearization with restriction enzymes which suggested that these sister bands differ from main products only in topological properties. Based on a previous report 1, we believed the sister catenane has a topological cross of 2.

Figure 1 b and c illustrate the experiment results of revised designs (with TT spacer). As shown in Figure 1b, two-ring catenanes were hardly observed in lane 4-6 and yield of DNA Triad in lane 7 (50.5%) improved greatly than the former one. Comparing with Figure 1a, the two-ring catenanes of Lk 2 were not observed. Though catenanes A2B and A2C (lane 4 and 5 in Figure 1c) can be detected, the yield (49.4%) of A2BC is also acceptable.

Fabrication of DNA Olympic Rings


Figure 2 Gel pattern of five-ring catenane.
Using the strategy of making DNA Triad, we successfully fabricated DNA Olympic Rings. The result is shown in Figure 2. Compared with normal method of making DNA catenanes, our method is of high efficiency and controllability. Lane 16 contains five-ring catenane made in traditional method in which all linear single strands were added simultaneously and ligated to form catenanes. As the picture shows, catenanes containing more than two rings can hardly be seen in the lane. Making use of ‘leading strand’, shown in lane 17, the reaction products differed dramatically from the former one. It was easy to tell the position of different catenanes. Although the yield (c.a. 16.6%) was not as high as DNA Triad,we are, to our knowledge, the first team making DNA catenane containing more than three rings.

 

 

 

Characterization of catenanes

As we mentioned above (see design), all products were first determined by exonuclease digestion . The connector region of each ring on catenane contains a restriction enzyme region. Restriction enzyme digestion was then carried out in order to prove the structure. Figure 3 illustrates that these products could be digested by corresponding enzymes and after digestion, offspring was observed as expect.

For DNA triad, first, the ligation products were characterized as DNA catenanes using exonuclease digestion methods. All ligated products could not be cleaved by Exonuclease I, which digests ssDNA from 3’ to 5’, Exonuclease III, which digests dsDNA from 3’ to 5’, or both (data not shown). Meanwhile, these products could be digested by corresponding restriction enzymes (Figure 3. Lane 2, 3 Because of the complexity of secondary structure，the A ring of Triad cannot be digested by restriction enzyme. We indirectly proved the existence of A ring by another experiment; the Triad cannot be fabricated in the absence of connector A, only two-ring catenane was observed on the PAGE gel.). These results suggested that DNA catenanes were cyclized without sticky ends.
